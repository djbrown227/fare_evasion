What’s Happening Now

Fare evasion on NYC buses has surged to record levels, with nearly half of all riders now boarding without paying. This shift has created a two-tier system, where paying passengers are often the minority. The increase—from around 20% pre-pandemic to over 44% today—reflects economic strain, limited enforcement, and outdated fare systems, especially on local routes. Despite efforts like EAGLE Teams and awareness campaigns, evasion remains high. These patterns suggest enforcement alone won’t solve the problem.

The Economics of Free Transit

While fare revenue represents a significant portion of transit funding, the total cost of fare collection—including infrastructure, enforcement, and administrative overhead—often consumes 15-25% of collected revenue.

When accounting for reduced operational complexity, improved service reliability, and increased ridership, the net financial impact of eliminating fares may be less dramatic than initially perceived.
